Quantum Computing

Overview:-

It's been awhile since the conjugate coding was invented by Stephen Wiesner (1960), later on, Alexander Holevo publishes a paper showing that n qubits can carry more than n classical bits of information,which was a crucial step to create powerful techniques to encrypt the data which eventually gave birth to quantum computing.In 2009 Qubit lifetime has been extended by a factor of 1000 and as time passed by, Quantum algorithm has been developed for differential equation systems.At the APS March 2018 conference, Google announced it has created a 72 qubit chip called Bristlecone which will serve as a basis for its demonstration of quantum supremacy, then , Intel confirms development of a 49-qubit superconducting test chip, called "Tangle Lake".This is followed by the company IonQ which introduced the first commercial trapped ion quantum computer, with a program length of over 60 two-qubit gates.

So what exactly is a quantum computer ?

A quantum computer is a machine that uses quantum mechanics to perform calculations.

How is this different from other computers?

Our computers are based on digital electronics and operate using bits and logic gates. A quantum computer, conversely, uses quantum mechanics to perform computation. Rather than bits and logic gates, quantum computers use qubits and quantum gates.

Hmm doesn't it sound amazing.Let’s start with what a qubit is. A qubit is a vector of two complex numbers with unit length.For starters, a bit is either a 0 or 1. There are no probabilities here, it’s either known to be a 0 or it’s known to be a 1. A qubit, conversely, is inherently probabilistic, meaning that two identical qubits may have different values once measured.

Qubit Control

Computer scientists control the microscopic particles that act as qubits in quantum computers by using control devices.

  • Ion traps use optical or magnetic fields (or a combination of both) to trap ions.
  • Optical traps use light waves to trap and control particles.
  • Quantum dots are made of semiconductor material and are used to contain and manipulate electrons.
  • Semiconductor impurities contain electrons by using "unwanted" atoms found in semiconductor material.
  • Superconducting circuits allow electrons to flow with almost no resistance at very low temperatures.
